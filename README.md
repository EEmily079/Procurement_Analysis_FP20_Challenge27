# Procurement_Analysis_FP20_Challenge27

Procurement Data Challenge
Procurement teams keep organizations running by purchasing the goods and services needed for daily operations. In reality, rising costs, supplier risk, delivery delays, and off-contract spending can create budget pressure and inefficiency. This analysis helps leaders understand spending patterns, monitor supplier performance, track savings, and identify compliance issues. With better visibility into procurement data, organizations can reduce risk, improve efficiency, and make more informed business decisions.

In this challenge, participants will work with a procurement dataset covering purchase order activity from 2022 to 2024. The dataset includes 5,200 procurement records and brings together information across purchasing transactions, suppliers, contracts, delivery performance, invoices, payments, budgets, and savings.

Key Questions to Explore

1.	How has procurement spend changed over time? 
Procurement spending shows seasonal fluctuations rather than a steady trend. Spending peaks vary by year, suggesting demand or purchase timing changes across departments and periods.
2.	Which categories, departments, suppliers, or regions account for the highest spend? 

3.	How does actual spend compare with budget? 
Actual spend is 29.84M below budget, or 8.8% under budget.

4.	Where are the biggest savings being achieved? 
The largest savings come from Europe, mainly driven by IT Software category savings. Marketing is the top department contributing to savings.

5.	Which suppliers are associated with the most spend, and how do they compare by risk level, status, tier, region, and ESG score? 
Top supplier spend is concentrated in Blue Horizon Packaging and Delta Engineering at 75M each. Comparing their risk, tier, region, and ESG status helps highlight supplier exposure and potential governance risks.

6.	Are there differences in performance between preferred and non-preferred suppliers, and how much spend is concentrated in single-source suppliers? 
High-spend suppliers are not always low-risk. Several major suppliers, including Nordic Office Solutions and Pacific Rim Supplies, show high invoice issue rates and large problem invoice amounts, indicating potential payment control and supplier performance risks.

7.	What is the on-time delivery rate, and which suppliers, categories, or departments experience the most delays? 
The overall on-time delivery rate is 64%, meaning around 36% of deliveries are delayed. Legal and Facilities experience the longest delays at 39% each, followed by HR at 37%. Supplier-level review is needed to identify whether delays are concentrated among specific high-spend or high-risk suppliers. 

8.	How do lead times vary across supplier types or categories, and are delays linked to higher-risk suppliers or certain contract types? 
Lead times are fairly consistent across supplier groups, but slightly longer delays appear in HR & Training, high-risk suppliers, and Master/Spot contracts. This suggests that supplier risk and contract type may have a small impact on delivery speed.

9.	How much maverick spend exists in the dataset, and how often are preferred suppliers not being used? 
11% of spend is maverick, and 67% is linked to non-preferred suppliers. Atlantic Raw Materials is the largest high-risk maverick supplier at 37M.

10.	What proportion of invoices are overdue, disputed, or unmatched, and where are the main warning signs in the procurement process?
Payment risk is visible across the process, with only 33.17% of invoices paid and 15.82% overdue. The biggest warning signs are high invoice issue rates and large problem invoice amounts, especially among Pacific Rim Supplies, Nordic Office Solutions, Iron Gate Steel, and Meridian Tech.
